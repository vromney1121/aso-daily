Today's Lead

Personal Intelligence goes global and free — and the GEO unit of analysis is no longer the brand

Google quietly extended Personal Intelligence — the personalization layer inside AI Mode that references a user's Gmail and Photos data — to nearly 200 countries and 98 languages, and removed the subscription gate. The implication is the hinge point of the week: a brand's presence inside a single user's Gmail (receipts, newsletters, calendar invites) is now a direct ranking input for that user's AI Mode answer. GEO programs aimed at the category level still matter, but the new optimization frontier is the slice of earned media that lands in inboxes. Owned newsletters, transactional email, partner mentions in B2B inboxes — assets the SEO team has historically ignored — just became measurable GEO inventory.

Top Stories

85% of brand mentions in AI answers come from third parties — not your site. AI engines learn about brands primarily from reviews, media coverage, and forums; owned sites contribute roughly 15% of the citation signal. Pair with Surfer SEO's finding that ~70% of URLs cited in AI Overviews never ranked in the top 10 organic results, and the entity-stack thesis stops being theory.

Barnard's "delegation boundary" gets the long-form treatment. Search Engine Land published a feature this week formalizing the framing: the moment a user delegates a buying decision to the machine is the moment competitors get filtered out before they ever surface. The accompanying four-point diagnostic (definition, consistency, credibility, sentiment) is the entity audit GEO consultants have been quietly selling; now it has a name CMOs will repeat.

And 50% of cited content is less than 13 weeks old — the recency requirement is the operationally important number this quarter.

Discussions Worth Catching

Share-of-voice as a primary metric is breaking. If 85% of citation signal comes from third parties and AI Mode personalizes against an inbox, brand-level dashboards measure the wrong unit. The KPI is share of personalized answers by audience segment that include the brand on the shortlist.

"AEO and GEO aren't separate disciplines" is procurement language. It gives CMOs cover to consolidate budget under a single program owner. Vendor positioning will compress accordingly — single-platform pitches will get more credible by end of summer.

The 13-week recency window has uncomfortable implications for evergreen content. The asset that earned the citation in February isn't earning it in May. PR and content cadence for GEO programs needs to be quarterly, not annual — and most agency retainers are not priced for that.
